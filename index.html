About TeNA

A neighbourhood watch for civic infrastructure

Tellapur Neighbourhood Association is a voluntary, non-commercial body formed by residents across Tellapur and Nallagandla to push for the basic civic amenities a fast-growing municipality keeps deferring — roads, streetlighting, drainage, water, and public safety.

We're not a gated-community welfare group. TeNA works across apartment complexes, gated villas and independent houses alike, and takes up issues with the municipality, Cyberabad police, HMWSSB, and the state government directly — often in public, and always with a paper trail.

Our president, Ramana Eshwaragari, has been the association's public face in dealings with the Chief Minister's office, the District Collector, and Telangana's pollution and water boards.

What we work on

Four fronts, one neighbourhood

TeNA's work splits into what residents actually raised at our meetings — not a boilerplate mission statement.

Civic Infrastructure

Roads, power & a fair tax bill

Chasing completion of the RR30 Extension road, the long-pending Tellapur electric substation, streetlighting and CCTV coverage, and a fairer, ARV-based property tax in place of the current land-value assessment.

"Richest municipality, poorest facilities" — TeNA's case to the state government

Environment & Lakes

Keeping Mellacheruvu alive

Organising lake clean-up drives with the Cyberabad Municipal Corporation, installing floating wetlands with Canna Indica plants for natural water purification, and formally flagging groundwater over-extraction and air pollution to the district authorities.

Complaint ID complaint_19657 — air pollution, filed with TGPCB

Health & Safety

Care that reaches every gate

Coordinating a free 24×7 ambulance service for Tellapur residents and organising free health screening camps, including a breast cancer screening drive that reached over 200 women in the Kollur 2BHK community.

Free ambulance service — dial-and-dispatch for all residents

FAQ

How TeNA works

Simple answers for residents who want to raise issues, volunteer, or understand how petitions move from complaint to closure.

Who can raise an issue through TeNA?

Any resident from Tellapur, Nallagandla, or neighboring communities can raise an issue. Apartment associations, villa communities, and independent house clusters are all included.

How is an issue tracked after it is reported?

TeNA records the issue with context, assigns an escalation path, and tracks follow-up through public updates and references in the Civic Ledger where available.

Does TeNA handle only complaints?

No. TeNA also coordinates preventive and community work, including clean-up drives, health camps, sports events, and safety initiatives.

How can I volunteer?

Follow TeNA channels, attend the next resident meeting, or message via X and Instagram with your community name and area of support.
